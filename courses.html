All Five Exam Domains. Covered.

We follow the official CompTIA SY0-701 objectives to the letter, with no outdated content. Every session maps directly to exam objectives.

12%
Domain 1 - General Security Concepts

The foundational layer. You'll learn the security control categories (technical, managerial, operational, physical), the CIA triad, Zero Trust architecture, cryptographic solutions including PKI and certificate management, and change management processes. We build your mental framework here so everything else clicks.

Includes: Control types · CIA / AAA / Non-repudiation · Zero Trust · Encryption & hashing · Digital signatures · PKI & certificates · Physical security

22%
Domain 2 - Threats, Vulnerabilities & Mitigations

The highest-weighted conceptual domain. You'll learn to identify threat actors (nation-states, insider threats, hacktivists, organized crime), analyze attack vectors, and recognize indicators of malicious activity. We cover every major attack type and the mitigation techniques used to stop them.

Includes: Threat actors & motivations · Social engineering (phishing, vishing, smishing) · Malware types · Network & application attacks · Vulnerability classes · Hardening techniques · Least privilege & segmentation

18%
Domain 3 - Security Architecture

How environments are designed to be secure. We cover cloud models and shared responsibility, network segmentation, secure communication (VPN, TLS, IPSec, SASE), data protection strategies, and resilience planning - including backup, recovery, and business continuity.

Includes: Cloud / hybrid / on-prem architecture · IaC & microservices · Firewalls & IDS/IPS · VPN & tunneling · Data classification & encryption · High availability · RTO / RPO · Disaster recovery

28%
Domain 4 - Security Operations Largest Domain

The most exam-heavy domain. This is where your practitioner instructor earns their living - covering real operational topics like vulnerability management, identity and access management, SIEMs, endpoint detection, incident response, and digital forensics. This is how security is actually done day-to-day.

Includes: Asset management · Vulnerability scanning & CVSS · IAM & MFA · SSO / SAML / OAuth / LDAP · SIEM & DLP · Endpoint hardening · Incident response lifecycle · Digital forensics · Automation & scripting

20%
Domain 5 - Security Program Management & Oversight

Governance, risk, and compliance, also known as the business side of security. You'll learn how organizations manage risk, handle third-party vendor assessments, maintain regulatory compliance, conduct audits, and build security awareness programs. Often underestimated; always on the exam.

Includes: Security governance & policies · Risk identification & analysis (SLE, ALE, ARO) · Risk register · Third-party risk & vendor management · Compliance frameworks · Audit types · Penetration testing concepts · Security awareness training
