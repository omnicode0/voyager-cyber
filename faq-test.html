Security+ Bootcamp FAQ

Everything you need to know about Voyager Cyber's live training program.

Do I need prior cybersecurity experience?

No. CompTIA recommends but does not require prior experience. This program is designed specifically for beginners and career changers. We start with the fundamentals and build systematically through all five exam domains. If you're comfortable using a computer and willing to put in the study time, you're ready.

Will this help me in my current IT job?

Yes. Security+ covers topics such as identity management, network security, vulnerability management, incident response, and risk management. These skills are increasingly valuable for help desk technicians, desktop support staff, system administrators, and other IT professionals.

Who is this program designed for?

This program is designed for career changers, IT professionals, healthcare IT staff, government employees, military personnel, and anyone looking to build a strong cybersecurity foundation. Whether you're entering the field or expanding your current responsibilities, the curriculum is designed to take you from the fundamentals through all Security+ exam domains.

Who is this program NOT for?

If you already have extensive cybersecurity experience, multiple security certifications, or a decade of broad IT experience across networking, systems, cloud, and security, you may be able to prepare for Security+ through self-study alone. Voyager Cyber is designed for students who value structure, instructor access, accountability, and a guided path to certification.

Will this help me get a job in cybersecurity?

Security+ is the most widely requested entry-level cybersecurity certification, with over 70,000 open roles actively listing it as a requirement. This program prepares you to pass the exam, achieve certification, and understand the material to get you job-ready. Career Accelerator tier students also receive resume review, career counseling, and interview prep to maximize their chances of landing a role. While experience is valued most highly across industries, Security+ remains a respected credential that serves as a valuable signal of general security knowledge and competence.

How much time do I need each week?

Plan for 7 hours of live instruction per week plus 7-14 hours of structured self-study. On the lower end for people with an IT background, on the upper end for career changers. That's roughly 14-21 hours per week total. This is demanding, but manageable for a working adult who is serious about making this transition.

What if I miss a live session?

Every session is recorded. If life gets in the way, you'll have full access to the recording so you never fall behind.

What if I fail the exam?

If you purchased your voucher through us and do not pass, we utilize the voucher's built-in retake to cover your second attempt. We will also provide a personalized study plan. If you fail the retake while meeting our documented eligibility requirements (80% live session attendance, completed practice exams, and two submitted score reports), you are eligible to attend another cohort at no additional cost. No additional vouchers are included. Full details in our Terms of Service.

How do I pick between the plans?

Starter gives you live instruction and materials. Professional, which most students choose, adds the exam voucher and retake support. Career Accelerator adds private coaching, resume review, and interview prep for those who want job-ready support beyond certification. Workforce is for businesses looking to train their employees. If you're unsure, Professional is the best starting point.

Why are we priced the way we are?

We keep pricing fair by running a focused, low-overhead program. Voyager Cyber is a veteran-owned small business — you're paying for live instruction from a practitioner, small cohorts, and a curriculum built entirely around Security+. No bloated catalog, no corporate overhead, no filler.

What studying style works best for this program?

This program is built for people who learn best with structure, live interaction, and accountability. If you've struggled with self-paced video courses or need someone to keep you on track, this format is designed for you. If you prefer fully self-directed study, our curriculum can still work - but the value is in the live sessions and instructor access.
